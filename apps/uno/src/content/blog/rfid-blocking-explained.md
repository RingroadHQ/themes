RFID blocking has become a standard feature in premium wallets. But what does it actually do, and do you really need it?

## What Is RFID?

RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) uses electromagnetic fields to identify and track tags attached to objects. Your contactless credit cards, debit cards, and some IDs contain RFID chips that transmit data when near a reader.

## The Risk

Digital skimming is real, though the risk depends on where you live and travel. A criminal with an RFID reader can potentially read your card data from several feet away - through your pocket, bag, or clothing. While the financial liability is typically with the bank, the hassle of dealing with fraudulent charges is yours.

## How RFID Blocking Works

RFID-blocking wallets contain a metallic lining (usually aluminum or copper) that creates a Faraday cage effect. This blocks electromagnetic signals from reaching your cards, preventing unauthorized reading.
